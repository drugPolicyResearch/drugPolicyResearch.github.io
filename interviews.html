Interviews

Summaries of interviews with important claims-makers in the Houston area, Rice University, and Texas A&M.

Katharine Neill Harris, Ph.D.
Alfred C. Glassell, III, Fellow in Drug Policy at the Baker Institute

Dr. Harris’s research is focused mainly on alternative drug policies and ways to reduce incarceration and address drug use as a public health issue rather than a criminal justice one. On topic of policies, she comments that people arrested on drug possession charges or drug related crimes may need drug treatment, but some may need other kinds of services. People who are arrested for this stuff often don’t have health care or an identification card. Those are barriers that make it difficult to get other things; we need services to address those other needs, not just diversion programs. Harris firmly supports the decriminalization of weed, stating that action as “a no brainer.” She further asserts that she doesn’t think there should be any penalties for marijuana use or sale. Rather, we should be smarter about how we do it and we should do it in a way that balances with public health. Looking to the future, Dr. Harris is optimistic about where current drug policy shifts are heading and sees drug treatment programs gaining more traction, especially in larger cities.
